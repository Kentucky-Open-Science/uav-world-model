\section*{1. PURPOSE}
To provide a status update on the development of LeWorldModel (LeWM), a predictive, localized physical AI architecture engineered for drone survivability. In head-on, closed-loop A$\to$B navigation tests against autonomous turrets (matched n=12), the LeWM-driven planner survives all 12 episodes (kill rate 0.000) where an oblivious baseline is killed every time (kill rate 1.000) and a detector-reactive baseline dies 11 of 12 (kill rate 0.917)[cite: 4]. By imagining future states rather than reacting to present detections, the system allows drones to anticipate and evade threats before they are fully visible[cite: 5].

\section*{2. BACKGROUND: WORLD MODELS VS. REACTIVE DETECTORS}
Current defense autonomous systems rely on reactive single-frame detectors, which inherently lack predictive lead time.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*, noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{The Reactive Failure Mode:} Single-frame danger detectors only process present frames[cite: 4]. By the time a traditional detector identifies a threat, its reactive flee fires only inside the kill zone, too late to escape the turret's lock — the detector-reactive baseline dies 11 of 12 (0.917 kill rate), nearly as bad as the oblivious baseline that flies straight in (1.000 kill rate)[cite: 4].
    \item \textbf{The World Model Advantage:} LeWM fundamentally differs by utilizing a Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture (JEPA)[cite: 2]. It uses a Vision Transformer (ViT) to map drone Point-of-View (POV) frames to a 192-dimensional latent space[cite: 2]. An action-conditioned transformer predictor then learns to forecast the \textit{next} frame's latent state based on a history of three context frames and actions[cite: 2].
    \item \textbf{Predictive Dynamics:} Imagination outperforms present-frame detection at forecasting danger across 0.5 to 2.0-second horizons[cite: 5]. This advantage stems from rolled-forward dynamics rather than an action-encoder shortcut[cite: 5].
\end{itemize}

\section*{3. SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE \& SIMULATION ENVIRONMENT}
Training and validation were executed in NVIDIA Isaac Sim utilizing domain-randomized urban scenes[cite: 2].

\section*{4. OPERATIONAL IMPACT \& LIVE RESULTS}
The system translates imagined future latents into survivability through a Cross-Entropy Method (CEM) planner.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*, noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Danger-Aware Planning:} The CEM planner rolls the world model's imagination forward over candidate 4-dimensional action sequences (vx, vy, vz, yaw\_rate) and selects the trajectory that minimizes the imagined danger logit[cite: 3].
    \item \textbf{Live Survivability:} In head-on A$\to$B navigation against autonomous turrets (matched n=12), the LeWM-driven planner achieved a kill rate of 0.000 and a survival rate of 1.000 --- the only controller to survive --- by imagining the turret from $\sim$9 m and holding back rather than entering the 8 m kill zone[cite: 4].
    \item \textbf{Safety:} The system executed evasive maneuvers with zero resulting crashes into surrounding urban infrastructure[cite: 4].
\end{itemize}
